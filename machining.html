Metal Machining.

Metal machining is the removal of material from a block, such as plastic or metal, resulting in a finished part. The process is known as subtractive manufacturing, executed through the use of motorised machine tools.

At Mighty-Tek we offer quick-turn machining that works well for both rapid prototyping and low or high volume end-use components. Our stock of several engineering materials and knowing which method to use at each stage of the project makes us stay ahead of schedule.

Turning Process

Turning is a machining process that uses a center lathe to create a detailed functional prototypes and end-use parts with cylindrical features, using a rod stock in a choice of metal materials which is rotated against live tooling to create final parts with axial and radial holes, flats, grooves and slots.

Turning process produces custom prototypes and end-use production parts in as fast as one day. When the run is complete, parts are inspected, boxed and shipped shortly thereafter.

Milling Process.

Milling is a machining process that uses rotary cutting tools, that move along multiple axes, to remove material from a block. Our Milling process can cut a choice of various engineering-grade thermoplastics and metals into complex shapes and precision components..

We have multiple tool sets based on efficient cutters for several materials. The machine then rapidly mills parts out of the block in a subtractive manufacturing process.

Surface Grinding.

The grinding Process is the conventional finish machining Process used for producing a good surface finish on the components and tightening up the tolerance.
